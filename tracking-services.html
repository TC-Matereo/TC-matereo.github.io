UNDERSEE FERRYBOX

Water Quality Monitoring for Marine Research & Operations

The Undersee FerryBox is a portable, water quality monitoring instrument designed for sailing, fishing and research vessels. It continuously collects water samples from a hull water inlet, measuring key parameters such as:

Temperature | pH | Dissolved Oxygen | Conductivity/Salinity | Turbidity | Chlorophyll-a | Phycoerythrin

WHY CHOOSE UNDERSEE FERRYBOX

✔ Seamless Integration: Easily mounts onto vessels via a hull ocean water inlet, requiring minimal installation time.

✔ Uninterrupted Data Logging: Collects and stores offline and online data, ensuring continuous environmental monitoring—even in remote locations.

✔ Zero Downtime Calibration: Swap out pre-calibrated units instantly—no onboard calibration required, reducing maintenance time.

✔ Autonomous Operation: Features a freshwater auto-cleaning system, delaying biofouling and reducing manual maintenance.

✔ Versatile Sensor Suite: Accurately measures Temperature, pH, Dissolved Oxygen, Conductivity/Salinity, Turbidity, and Chlorophyll-a for a comprehensive water quality analysis.

WHAT PARAMETERS DOES IT MEASURE?

Temperature – Detects oceanic fronts

pH – Monitors ocean acidification

Dissolved Oxygen – Crucial for aquatic life & fishery health

Conductivity/Salinity – Essential for oceanographic studies

Turbidity – Measures water clarity & sediment transport

Chlorophyll-a – Tracks algae growth

Phycoerythrin – Identifies cyanobacteria and red algae presence
